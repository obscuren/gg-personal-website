milestones:
  - year: "2013"
    title: "Co-founded Ethereum"
    description: "Helped build and design the world computer from scratch."
    expanded: "Banded with a group of likeminded individuals who believed decentralized computing could change the world. Wrote core protocol code, helped shape the architecture, and shipped something that went on to become a global platform. It was intense, chaotic, and foundational to everything that came after."

  - year: "2017"
    title: "Left Ethereum"
    description: "Time to move on. Burnt out.."
    expanded: "Ethereum was thriving, but my heart wasn't in blockchain anymore, the pressure took its toll. I'd been wanting to make games since before any of this started. Leaving was hard — but staying would have meant building someone else's dream instead of my own."

  - year: "2018"
    title: "Founded Grid Games"
    description: "Started a game studio with my brother Joey."
    expanded: "As little boys we said 'one day we'll build our own Game!'. In 2018 we finally did it — registered the company, set up the infrastructure, and committed. Two brothers, one vision: build games we actually want to play. No investors telling us what to make. No compromise on the creative direction."

  - year: "2019"
    title: "First Prototype"
    description: "\"Grid was born.\" — the first playable prototype in Unity."
    expanded: "April 3rd, 2019. The commit message said it all: \"Grid was born.\" We were baking city scenes, pushing wall and tower assets, doing terrain work. It was rough and ugly, but it was the first time the idea felt real. Before that commit, Grid Games was a conversation. After it, Grid Games was a product."

  - year: "2020"
    title: "COVID"
    description: "Stayed lean. Kept building through the uncertainty."
    expanded: "The pandemic made everything uncertain. We wanted to hire, to scale, to move faster — but the world wasn't cooperating. So we stayed lean, just the two of us, and kept iterating. In hindsight, that constraint forced discipline. Every decision had to count."

  - year: "2020"
    title: "Unity Rebuild"
    description: "Started fresh with GridOnline-Deux. Character systems, login, networking."
    expanded: "October 2020 — we started over. The prototype proved the concept, but we needed a proper foundation. Within weeks we had character creation, login systems, selection screens, and networking components. This wasn't a prototype anymore. This was the real build."

  - year: "2022"
    title: "Moved to UE5"
    description: "Made the call to switch to Unreal Engine 5."
    expanded: "The hardest decision we made. Years of Unity work, thrown away. But UE5 had what we needed for the long term — the rendering pipeline, the tooling, the scalability. Sometimes you have to abandon working code to build something better. October 10th, 2022: first commit in the new repo. A fresh start."

  - year: "2026"
    title: "ASTRA"
    description: "Built a sci-fi roguelike in C++ — in a couple of days with Claude."
    expanded: "A side project born from wanting to just build something for fun. ASTRA is a sci-fi roguelike dungeon crawler that runs in the terminal. ASCII graphics, procedural galaxies, cave generation, combat — all built in a couple of evenings with Claude as a coding partner. It reminded me why I started programming in the first place."
